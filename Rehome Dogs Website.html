At Paws and Home, we understand that the decision to rehome your dog can be incredibly challenging and emotional. Our Rehoming Process

  1. Initial Consultation:

    • We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your dog's history, behavior, health, and specific needs. This helps us gather important information to ensure the best possible match with a new family.

  2. Profile Creation:

    • Based on the information provided, we create a detailed profile for your dog, including photos and a description highlighting their personality, preferences, and any special requirements. This profile will be featured on our website and shared with potential adopters.

  3. Health and Behavior Assessment:

    • Our team of veterinarians and behavior specialists conduct a comprehensive health check and behavior assessment to identify any medical or behavioral issues that need to be addressed before rehoming.

  4. Matchmaking:

    • We utilize our extensive network and database to find potential adopters who match the specific needs and characteristics of your dog. Our goal is to ensure each dog is placed in a caring and suitable environment where they can thrive.

  5. Adoption Screening:

    • Potential adopters undergo a rigorous screening process, including background checks, home visits, and interviews. We ensure that adopters are fully prepared and committed to providing a loving and permanent home.

  6. Trial Period:

    • To ensure a good fit, we offer a trial period where the dog can spend time in the prospective adopters home. This helps both the dog and the family adjust and ensures compatibility before finalizing the adoption.

  7. Finalization:

    • Once both parties are confident in the match, we complete the necessary paperwork and provide resources and support for a smooth transition.

  • Emotional Support:

    • We understand that rehoming your pet is a difficult decision. Our team is here to offer emotional support and guidance throughout the process, ensuring you feel comfortable and informed every step of the way.

    • We provide a range of resources, including articles, videos, and workshops on topics such as dealing with the emotions of rehoming, preparing your dog for a new home, and what to expect during the transition period.

    • If immediate rehoming isnt possible, we offer temporary care options, including foster homes, to ensure your dog is safe and well-cared-for while we find the perfect match.

JavaScript (scripts.js)

javascript
document.addEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', (event) => {
    // Get the modal
    var modal = document.getElementById("modal");

    // Get the button that opens the modal
    var btn = document.getElementById("rehomingBtn");

    // Get the <span> element that closes the modal
    var span = document.getElementsByClassName("close")[0];

    // When the user clicks the button, open the modal
    btn.onclick = function() {
        modal.style.display = "block";
    }

    // When the user clicks on <span> (x), close the modal
    span.onclick = function() {
        modal.style.display = "none";
    }

    // When the user clicks anywhere outside of the modal, close it
    window.onclick = function(event) {
        if (event.target == modal) {
            modal.style.display = "none";
        }
    }
});

  • HTML: Sets up the structure of the page with a button to open the modal and the modal itself.
  • CSS: Styles the page, button, and modal for a clean look.
  • JavaScript: Adds interactivity to open and close the modal.
